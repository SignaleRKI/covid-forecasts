Evaluation

We use two different measures to assess predictive performance.

  • The absolute error (AE) serves to assess the accuracy of the point forecast (typically the predictive median).
  • The weighted interval score (WIS) assesses all prediction intervals (or quantiles) simulataneously and thus also takes into account the uncertainty of a distribution. It can be decomposed into three components representing the spread of forecast distributions and penalties for over- and underprediction.

For both measures lower values are better. An interactive app allowing you to explore the evaluation results for all models is available here.

Detailed information can be found in this preprint:

  • Bracher, Ray, Reich and Gneiting: Evaluating epidemic forecasts in an interval format.

Research

The German and Polish COVID-19 Forecast Hub is embedded into a prospective forecast evaluation study. In October 2020 we deposited a study protocol laying out the exact procedures here. Results from the first evaluation period, which stretches from October to mid-December 2020, will be made available around the end of the year.

We are moreover closely collaborating with our colleagues from the US COVID-19 Forecast Hub. First results from this project (co-authored by members of our team) can be found here:

  • Ray et al: Ensemble Forecasts of Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19) in the U.S.
  • Brooks, Ray, Bien, Bracher, Rumack, Tibshirani, Reich: Comparing ensemble approaches for short-term probabilistic COVID-19 forecasts in the U.S.
